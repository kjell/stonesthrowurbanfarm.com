About the Farmers

From a young age, Alex Liebman has been interested in getting dirty, staying dirty, and eating well. After spending time on rural vegetable, fruit and livestock operations in Massachusetts and California, he is excited to be figuring out how to farm in the city. He wants to develop sustainable urban food systems that work in conjunction with rural farms to provide fresh, healthy produce to all people. Alex also spends time working with the Latino Economic Development Center, helping Latino-led farming cooperatives begin successfully throughout the state. When not farming, you can find Alex playing the saxophone, playing pick-up hockey on the city's rinks, or with his eyes pinned to decaying trees searching for birds.

Emily Hanson transplanted herself to the Twin Cities after spending the first 18 years of her life in a small town in New Hampshire. She grew up helping tending a big vegetable garden in the summers and spending every spare moment in the woods behind her house, then fled the coop and moved to Minnesota for college. As an Environmental Studies major and Geography minor at Macalester College, she studied a little of everything under the sun. She wants to dedicate her life to creating resilient communities- through urban and rural sustainable agriculture, neighborhood skill sharing, alternative education, and all manner of working with her hands. She loves the way farming makes you feel rich even when your pockets are filled with dirt instead of money.

Eric Larsen grew up on a small Christmas tree and blueberry farm in Forest Lake, MN. He earned a degree in finance and nonprofit management from the University of Minnesota in 2008. Mr. Larsen now lives in the Phillips neighborhood of South Minneapolis where he's active in the Transition Town movement to build resilient communities. Eric is a proud alumni of the AmeriCorps NCCC program where he served on projects with nonprofits everywhere from Louisiana to Maine. He's interested in growing food, building community with friends and neighbors, cooking, community service, dancing, and experiencing other cultures, all of which can be found within a short venture from his own home!

Klaus Zimmermann-Mayo grew up in the heart of South Minneapolis, on 17th Avenue. He spent his childhood tagging along to his dad's handyman jobs, conducting scientific experiments, and digging holes in the backyard. With handyman skills acquired from his dad, he's spent most of his adult life repairing houses, fences, sheds, and just about anything else that needs fixing. Upon meeting Emily, Alex, and Robin in the spring of 2011, his budding interest in farming grew into a full-fledged passion. He spent 2012 working and living in Decorah Iowa at a diversified seed-saving homestead, and working at a neighboring organic dairy farm. When he's not working, he likes to bang on the drums and experiment with fermentation.

Robin Major grew up on a sustainable sheep cheese farm in Putney, Vermont and spent many summers milking sheep and throwing hay bales. Since leaving the farm, she has built upon her childhood experiences by studying biology at Macalester College and spending summers working on other farms and garden projects. Since graduating, she has started a small urban farm business and spent a season working at Common Harvest Farm in Osceola, Wisconsin. She adores perennial flowers, herbs, grasses, and medicines, and is the Lady of the Greenhouse. Outside of farming, she enjoys long runs and big meals.

John Seitz grew up in Hopkins, MN. He fled the coop to educate himself at the University of Madison, then at the organic farming program in Santa Cruz, California. A master of the knife, he now works as a butcher at The Wedge. He began urban farming in 2008, and was a founder of Uptown Farmers. A man of many talents, he works part-time with the farm, and keeps us well fed and on our toes.
